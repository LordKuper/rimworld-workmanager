Work Manager — Concept

6 sections · RimWorld 1.1–1.6 · reverse-engineered from existing mod

Vision

Work Manager is a RimWorld mod that takes over the tedious, error-prone job of micromanaging colonist work. It automatically and continuously assigns each pawn's work-type priorities — and, optionally, their daily schedules — based on skills, passions, learning rates, health and configurable per-work-type rules, so the colony keeps the right people on the right jobs as it grows and changes without the player re-balancing the work tab by hand.

Target users

  • Players who dislike work-tab micromanagement — want colonists' work priorities kept sensible automatically instead of manually retuning the work tab every time the colony changes.
  • Players of larger or fast-changing colonies — need scheduling and dedicated-worker coverage to scale across many pawns, day/night shifts and night-owl colonists without manual upkeep.
  • Players who tune colony behavior in depth — want fine-grained control via per-work-type rules, allowed-worker filters, dedicated-worker modes, passion/learning-rate priorities and scoring weights, with per-pawn opt-out.
  • Modded-game users — run alongside other work and skill mods and expect Work Manager to integrate cleanly rather than conflict.

Value proposition

Work Manager removes the constant chore of hand-tuning the work tab. It periodically recomputes priorities so the most skilled, passionate or fastest-learning colonists are kept on each job, ensures critical work types always have enough coverage through configurable dedicated-worker rules, fills idle and leftover work, and can manage daily schedules into coordinated shifts (keeping partners together and covering key skills across shifts). It stays flexible — per-work-type rules, allowed-worker filters and per-pawn opt-out — and integrates with popular work and skill mods rather than fighting them.

Pillars

  • Automatic & continuousPriorities (and optionally schedules) recompute on a configurable cadence as the colony changes, with no manual re-tuning.
  • Skill-aware assignmentWork is steered to the most suitable colonists using skill level, passion and learning rate, with weighted scoring for dedicated workers.
  • Guaranteed coveragePer-work-type rules ensure minimum workers and dedicated-worker counts so essential jobs are never left unstaffed.
  • Configurable & overridablePer-work-type rules, allowed-worker filters, priority/threshold sliders and per-pawn opt-out toggles keep the player in control.
  • Plays well with othersDedicated compatibility shims let it integrate with other work and skill mods instead of conflicting.

Anti-Pillars

  • Not a work-tab UI replacementIt automates assignments rather than reimplementing the work tab; where a work-tab mod is present it defers to that mod's priority API.
  • Not a forced takeoverManaged pawns and managed schedules can be toggled off per pawn, and schedule management is opt-in per pawn and globally.
  • Not for non-player or non-working pawnsMechanoids, animals, prisoners and pawns that never work are excluded from management.

Constraints

  • Platform: RimWorld mod, packageId LordKuper.WorkManager; supports RimWorld 1.1 through 1.6.
  • Dependencies: requires Harmony (brrainz.harmony) on all versions; additionally requires LordKuper.Common on RimWorld 1.6.
  • Optional integrations (load-after): WorkTab, More Than Capable, PriorityMaster, Vanilla Skills Expanded.
